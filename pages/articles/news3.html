Baguio City Youth groups march their way for ML51

Photos by Joshua Daniel David, Sheryn Dinamling, and Angela Tecson

by Joana Mae Gomez

September 22, 2023

Share
Tweet

With the theme “Martial Law Noon, Terror Law Ngayon,” the 51st Commemoration of Martial Law filled the roads of Baguio City with hundreds of students and activists from local and university-wide organizations last September 21, Thursday.

The day-long program started with a Martial Law Exhibit held at Malcolm Square, and ended with a march rally from the Baguio City Post Office to Malcolm Square. However, the route of the march was cut short after participants were met with the police’s truncheon barricade at Magsaysay Avenue corner General Luna where negotiations with the police were causing traffic and disputes with some bystanders.

“Naiintindihan po natin na may mga pagtingin talagang gano’n sa mga pagkilos na ‘to, at ang punto naman po ng kilusan ay hindi maging antagonistic sa kanila, sa mga normal na taong hindi naiintindihan ang ating pagkilos. Kaya naman po inaabot natin sila, ine-explain natin sa kanila kung ano nga ba ang essence, ano nga bang pinupuso ng ating pagkilos. Pero s’yempre ibang [usapan] na ‘yong mga pulis at militar, dahil tunay naman pong mekanismo sila ng estado para supilin tayo. Ang aking statement na ‘yon ay para sa regular na tao lang naman talaga,” Saint Louis University student Ashli de Castro urged.

De Castro also proudly mentioned the fruits of their constant efforts of tapping and welcoming students into voicing out their opinions, “Natututo tayo. Kino-correct naman natin ‘yong mga pagkakamali natin before na hindi nila nararamdaman na welcome sila sa ating pagkilos — mas nilalapit natin ‘yong sarili natin sa kanila, at talagang pina-patunayan natin na kasama sila palagi sa ating pagkilos at sila ang puso ng ating pagkilos,” de Castro said.

The event also addressed the current issues under the administration of the dictator’s son Ferdinand “Bongbong” Marcos Jr. and Vice President Sara Duterte, especially with the worsening economic and political crises in the country.

“Ano ba ang sabi ni Sara Duterte na kasalanan ng mga raliyista kung bakit may mga dropout? Ang dahilan kung bakit maraming drop out ay dahil hindi abot kaya ang edukasyon,” Ned Tuguinay, Cordillera Peoples Alliance (CPA) spokesperson said.

Tuguinay also addressed the confidential and intelligence funds of President Marcos Jr. and Vice President Sara Duterte, who both refuse to answer the concerns raised by progressive lawmakers. “Ang nais nila ay tahimik nilang maibulsa at manakawan ang mamamayan,” Tuguinay expressed.

The program ended with a candle-lighting and a singing session by the participants of the said protest.
